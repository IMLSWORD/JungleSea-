Gandoca-Manzanillo National Wildlife Refuge

Gandoca-Manzanillo is a mixed National Wildlife Refuge (state and private), where the combination of land, beach, and sea landscapes, along with local culture, makes the site very attractive to visitors. The conservation and sustainable management of these ecosystems is carried out in parallel with the participation of local communities, where the habitat of threatened species such as manatees and sea turtles is protected. Its most important ecosystems (environments, biosphere) include its yellow sand beaches and wetlands: swamps, lagoons, flooded forests, reefs, seagrass beds, and algae. The refuge protects about 5 km2 of reef off Punta Uva, Manzanillo, and Mona. Coral species include brain corals and sea fans. It also protects the only natural mangrove oyster (Crassostrea rhizophorae) bank in the reef area of the coastal strip. In addition to wildlife, especially species that are endangered or with reduced populations.
